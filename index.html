biography:


Parham Behzad, Composer based in Berlin, his works performed by LUX:NM ensemble, Michael Wendeberg was born and raised in Tehran, Iran, in 1994. At the age of 7, he initiated his musical journey by playing Tombak, an Iranian percussion instrument. A year later, he developed an interest in classical music and he began receiving piano lessons. As he grew older, his musical interests expanded to experimental electronic music, and western contemporary music.

Initially pursuing a degree in Mechanical Engineering at BNUT in Babol, Iran, he decided to follow his musical interests after three years. He enrolled in Piano studies at UAST University in Tehran under the tutelage of Martyna Kosecka. Additionally, he received private compositional lessons from Kaveh Djodat, Idin Samimi Mofakham, and Mehdi Kazerouni.

In October 2019, Parham began his Bachelor of Composition studies at the Barenboimsaid Akademie in Berlin, where he currently studies in the studio of Prof. Stephan Winkler. He has also had had the privilege of learning from exceptional artists like Jörg Widmann, Arnulf Hermann, Georg Friedrich Haas, Chaya Czernowin, and Gilbert •Nouno.

▷_ SIMIYYA - for 6 Musicians - [2023]

↓

flute, bassoon, bass trombone, 3x 5-strings double basses

---> premiered on 9th of July 2023, Pierre Boulez Saal - Berlin.

Program note: {The piece consists of a mantra, an embodiment of sacred empathy woven into the music. Through a ceremonial process, wearing a flowing black robe while I am writing it, I seek to shatter my own antiquated talisman. In that indeterminate moment, where conquest remains uncertain, the art emerges, born from the depths of darkness...}

Additional notes:
The last part of the piece should be performed in the darkest light possible without conductor to conduct the piece. The score in this section written in free form but each musical ideas in each instruments will gives cues to each other in the darkness.

Watch here ➚


Simiyya, an ancient doctrine shrouded in secrecy, a connection between superior and inferior natures is sought. This practice, prevalent in the lands of Persia and Arabia, delves into the untapped powers of celestial bodies. As practitioners delve deeper into this enigmatic art, they master the manipulation of letters, names, and words, conjuring intense and vivid mental images that blur the line between reality and illusion. With each invocation, they unlock the hidden doorways of the mind, unlocking secrets that could change everything.

In “SIMIYYA” I took a Persian mode named “Shur” and I was inspired by its structure and how they used to play it in different ways, where in each “Gûshe,” the importance of notes becomes different. So, here I was dealing with a constant floating hierarchy of notes. I used the mode in my pitch organisation, Besides that I employed this amazing feature of this tradition in developing form of my piece. For pitches and harmony, I juxtaposed the intervals in “Shur” with a few equal temperament pitch classes. And later on, I crafted two different types of spectral harmonies: one that I created from “Shur” irregular ratios, and the other from the pitch classes with ordinary ratios.

Prominent Testimonial on the Composition:

“ Beyond the enthralling instrumentation he has chosen, the sounds he coaxed from the ensemble were truly extraordinary, magically converging in perfect timing and harmony. ”

Jörg Widmann
